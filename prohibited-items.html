Prohibited Items for International Shipping

Prohibited shipments refer to items, goods, or materials that cannot be transported (imported, exported, or otherwise moved) due to legal restrictions in at least one of the countries involved in the shipment. This includes items such as alcohol and tobacco, as well as high-value items that cannot be repaired or replaced. Additionally, certain goods are designated as prohibited by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO).

List of Prohibited Items

  • Aerosols
  • Any items banned by law in the country of origin, transit, or destination, such as ivory.
  • Counterfeit goods infringing on intellectual property rights
  • Tax stamps
  • Biological agents and infectious substances
  • Commemorative coins, medals, and high-value collectibles, including rare books, trading cards, and memorabilia.
  • Counterfeit currency
  • Drugs and other illegal substances (including medicinal cannabis and mushrooms).
  • Dry ice
  • Engines, generators, and automotive parts containing fuel or oil
  • Explosives and fireworks
  • Fire extinguishers
  • Firearms and their components, as well as military ammunition and replicas
  • All food products (including dried mushrooms)
  • Other perishable items
  • Gas cylinders and compressed gas
  • Hazardous waste, including used syringes and medical waste
  • Flammable and hazardous materials (according to IATA guidelines)
  • Human organs, embryos, and remains, including ashes
  • Hunting trophies and animal parts, such as skin and bones
  • Items with pornographic or offensive content
  • Live animals, including mammals, reptiles, fish, birds, and insects
  • Magnets and items containing ferromagnetic materials
  • Money, including cash, banknotes, coins, and financial instruments
  • Oil
  • Precious metals and loose gemstones
  • Prescription and non-prescription medications when shipped outside the EU
  • Empty medication containers and labels
  • Psychoactive substances
  • Seat belts
  • Self-inflating life jackets
  • Single airbags
  • Single batteries
  • Tobacco and its products, including electronic cigarettes
  • Undeclared shipments or those requiring regulatory approval
  • Unknown items
  • Improperly packaged or unpackaged items
  • Wet ice and any items that are damp, leaking, or emitting odors

Compliance with Laws and Standards

Shipping any product without the necessary documentation and approvals is prohibited. It is also illegal to transmit data or upload materials containing viruses or harmful software that could disrupt computer systems. All contributions must adhere to applicable laws in both the origin and destination countries. Additionally, submissions should not include:

  • Defamatory material
  • Obscene or offensive content
  • Promotion of violence or discrimination
  • Violations of intellectual property rights

Non-Compensation Items in International Shipping

Non-compensation items are goods that, while not explicitly prohibited, lack any form of coverage:

Unpacked Luggage

This includes suitcases, bags, and other types of luggage that do not have proper external packaging, such as plastic wrap, cardboard boxes, or packaging for collectibles and toys.

Glass Items

This category covers objects made from glass, including windows, aquariums, urns, mirrors, vases, glass frames, and any other glass products.

Inadequate Packaging

Any items that are placed in a package without adhering to our specified packaging guidelines are considered inadequately packaged.

Returned Goods

This includes damaged products and machinery that have been returned to the seller.
