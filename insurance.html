Shipment Insurance

Shipments are categorized into two types: documents and parcels.

Documents

Insurance Details for Documents

Customers are required to review the packaging instructions for documents to ensure the safety and security of the shipment, thereby facilitating a smooth and satisfactory shipping experience.

The customer will receive compensation limited to the shipping fees only.

The customer may be compensated up to $100. The exact compensation amount will be determined by the shipping company after a detailed explanation of the lost documents and their contents is provided.

Whether the shipment is insured or not, a claim must be initiated to investigate the damage and request compensation. The customer must provide a photo of the shipped documents, along with an image of the packaging, including the envelope used for shipping. Note that proper packaging is the customer's responsibility. Upon receiving the envelope from the courier, the recipient must confirm any damage to the documents and file a complaint at the time of signing for the delivery. Additionally, they should take a photo of the external envelope and the damaged documents to illustrate the extent of the damage. Based on this information, the shipping company will review all submitted data and images to process the compensation request. Accordingly, the shipping company will determine the available compensation based on the level of damage, and the final decision will rest with them

Parcel

Parcel Insurance

Customers are advised to review the packaging instructions for parcels to ensure the safety of the shipment and achieve a successful shipping experience.

Typically, non-personal items require an attached invoice indicating their value, which is necessary for customs clearance in the destination country. The insurance coverage is determined by the value stated on this invoice. For instance, if the invoice lists a value of $10, the insurance will match that amount. It is important to note that the maximum coverage limit is $10,000. If the stated value exceeds this amount, only $10,000 will be covered. Insurance is effective only in cases of loss.

Claims Procedures for Damage

If a shipment is damaged, the customer must follow these steps to initiate an investigation and request compensation:

Proof of Damage

The recipient must provide official evidence of the extent of the damage, which includes signing off on the delivery and filing a complaint.

Packaging Photos

The sender should provide images of both the internal and external packaging of the contents and the box before delivery.

Parcel Photos

Upon receipt, the recipient must send photos of the parcel from both the outside and inside, along with clear images of the damaged contents to assess the extent of the damage.

Based on the submitted information, the shipping company will review the case. If the packaging is deemed adequate, compensation will be issued according to the value of the contents. However, if the packaging is insufficient, the shipping company reserves the right to deny compensation or provide only partial reimbursement, based on the decision of the relevant management.

Important Note:

Please be aware that insurance does not cover any items that are fragile or susceptible to breaking.
