Packing and Labeling Standards

According to GoZagel's usage policy, the responsibility for packaging lies entirely with the customer.

Below are detailed guidelines to ensure your documents are properly packaged before shipping.

Document Packaging

Basic Packaging Requirements

1

Envelopes

Customers must place the documents intended for shipping inside a white A4-sized envelope.

Important Note : The envelope must be sealed in front of the courier, as the contents need to be verified before sealing.

2

Plastic Folders

Some customers prefer using an A4 plastic folder to protect the contents from moisture. However, there is a risk of papers easily falling out.

3

Recommendation

It is advisable to place the plastic folder inside an additional sealed A4 envelope, as shown in the attached images.

Size Considerations

• For documents measuring A3 or other sizes, appropriate envelopes must be used. Please note that the size of the package affects shipping costs, as shipping fees are calculated based on volumetric weight using the following formula: - Volumetric Weight: (Length * Width * Height) / 5000

Weight Guidelines

Less than 1 kg The envelopes shown in the images are not recommended for shipments weighing less than 1 kg to avoid additional charges based on size.

1 to 2 kg Envelopes can be used for documents weighing between 1 kg and 2 kg.

More than 2 kg A box must be used for packaging documents that exceed 2 kg, and the dimensions of the box (length, width, height) must be disclosed, as size also impacts cost.

Package Shipping Guidelines

Below are important instructions to follow when shipping packages to ensure the safety of the contents and to avoid any issues during the shipping process.

Packaging Restrictions

1

Gift Boxes

The use of gift boxes is prohibited, as they are easy to open and can easily lose their contents. Some customers believe that wrapping gift boxes with materials like stretch film can enhance security, but this is incorrect. The packaging will be opened at the airport for content verification and will not be resealed.

2

Compressed Cardboard

Packaging with compressed cardboard is not acceptable, as verification processes require opening the box, which may lead to damage to the contents or explosion of the box due to pressure. Additionally, using a box that is significantly larger than its contents may cause the box to fold and become damaged.

3

Reconstructed Boxes

Some customers try to reduce shipping costs by cutting or assembling pieces of cardboard to form a smaller box. This is unacceptable as it exposes the box to the risk of tearing during transport, which can result in the loss of contents.

Note:

: The improper methods mentioned above will lead to additional packaging by the shipping team to protect the contents, affecting the box size and shipping costs. Volumetric weight is calculated as follows: Volumetric Weight = (Length * Width * Height) / 5000

Proper Packaging Requirements

1

Basic Requirements Rechargeable

The packaging must be in a box with four corners, making it easy to open and close during inspection. The box must not be torn, compressed, or reconstructed.
Note: If there are brand markings on the box, the customer must obscure them in a way that does not damage the box

3

Labels

It is advisable to add a clear label on the box indicating its fragility. This label should be printed by the sender and affixed to the corners to ensure safe transport of the shipment.
